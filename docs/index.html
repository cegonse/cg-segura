Hi, I'm César González

I'm a software developer from Valencia, Spain and this is my portfolio. I graduated on communication engineering and right now I work as a software engineer at Casa Systems, developing next-gen mobile broadband solutions. I have worked on computational genomics and game development in the past, and I keep a great interest on both.

My main interests are embedded and low level software development, feeling the most confortable as close to the hardware as possible. I also enjoy tool development, where I try to contribute to the open source scene, and videogame development, which I do in my spare time as a hobby.

Following is some of my recent work, and the work I'm most fond of. I hope you can make good use of it, and don't hesitate to contact me if you have any question.

Research

These are the most relevant research results from my time working in the GREV research group at the University of Valencia. The research was focused on DNA read mapping, methylation and hydroximethylation analysis for cancer research and diagnosis, in tandem with the medical institute INCLIVA. Besides this, there is also the research results from my Bs. C. thesis in computer graphics, where I worked on finding novel methods for improving terrain rendering performance.

Name Description / Publisher Link / DOI
On the Use of Binary Trees for DNA Hydroxymethylation Analysis Springer International Conference on Algorithms and Architectures for Parallel Processing https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-65482-9_37
HPG-Methyl An ultrafast and highly sensitive Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) read mapper and methylation extractor Source code and binaries
